Due Tomorrow

Made in Procreate

Summary

Originally created as a promotional comic for Ka Manaʻo’s social media, this piece became an opportunity to experiment with new approaches to short-form comic storytelling.

In the early stages, I explored using dialogue-heavy speech bubbles to introduce the story in the first panel. However, I found that this approach felt overly expository and disrupted the pacing. This led me to shift toward a more visual-first storytelling method, relying on composition and environmental cues rather than explicit narration.

From an art direction standpoint, I intentionally designed the comic so that the visual atmosphere evolves throughout the panels, transitioning from a simple, minimal style to a more detailed and dramatic reveal for the punchline, with background elements subtly change over time to support that progression. This concept influenced my decision to use a cutesy, simplified character style at the start, allowing the shift in detail to feel more impactful.

Although this is one of the simplest projects I’ve created, it strengthened my understanding of panel composition, pacing, and visual hierarchy. The experience highlighted the importance of guiding the viewer’s attention through line weight, contrast, and focal emphasis, particularly when distinguishing key elements such as the main character and the laptop. I look forward to further refining these techniques in future narrative-driven pieces.
