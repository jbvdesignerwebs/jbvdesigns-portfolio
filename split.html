s.p.l.i.t. (Fan Art)

Tools: Art & See Perspective Tool, MagicPoser, Procreate and Adobe Illustrator

Timeline: 1 month (December 2025)

Summary

Before departing from Ka Manaʻo, I wrote a mini-review on an indie game I enjoyed when it released, titled “s.p.l.i.t.” (developed by Mike Klubnika). It’s a psychological horror typing game where you play as a rogue hacker navigating the shady, massive organization you work for.

This illustration was one of the few pieces where I focused on creating a fully rendered and detailed composition. For the final piece, my goal was to include story elements that hint at the narrative without spoiling it, so that after playing the game, readers can recognize how the illustration ties everything together.

Throughout the process, I experimented with new tools and techniques. I used a perspective grid from Art & See by Luciano Drehmer for the background angle, MagicPoser to define the angle of the main character’s face, and Adobe Illustrator to create and distort the Command Prompt visuals. The full rendering, coloring, linework, and compositing were completed in Procreate.

This project involved a lot of trial and error, where I had to slow down and focus on detail and atmosphere, even on a deadline. Overall, it was a rewarding learning experience that helped strengthen both my technical workflow and my approach to fully rendered illustration, and it’s a style I hope to continue exploring in future projects.
