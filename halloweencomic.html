Late Night Visitor

[Content Warning: Uncanny/Creepy Faces]

Tool: Procreate

Timeline: 2 weeks (October 2024)

Background

While working for Ka Manaʻo, the team aimed to create seasonal social media content that would attract more engagement to the publication. For Halloween, I proposed a couple of comic concepts, including a 10-panel horror-comedy story designed specifically for Instagram.

Prior to Process

Although I had experience with illustration, I had never created a comic before — especially not within the horror genre. I’ve always been inspired by surreal horror, particularly the atmospheric storytelling of Junji Ito’s Uzumaki. However, since this comic was for Ka Manaʻo’s social media, it was important that the tone remained appropriate and aligned with the brand. I wanted to experiment with horror elements while ending the story on a lighthearted twist that felt consistent with the publication’s voice.

Project Process

Writing and Sketching

During brainstorming, I focused on balancing suspense with humor. Because this was my first horror comic, I intentionally kept the story simple and clear. The goal was to create tension visually while ensuring the ending felt fun and approachable.

After presenting my concept sketches to the Ka Manaʻo team, I received approval to move forward with the full comic.

While developing the panels, I experimented with texturing, lighting, and shading to create atmosphere. I initially tried cross-hatching techniques inspired by manga styles, but due to time constraints, I transitioned to a cleaner shading style.

Panel 6 was also a technical challenge, as it required drawing a low-angle perspective outside my comfort zone. I practiced using hand-drawn perspective guides and 3D references before constructing a rectangular framework to establish accurate proportions. This method ultimately helped me achieve a more convincing composition.

Finalizing

To maintain stylistic consistency, I completed the sketch and line art for each panel sequentially before moving into coloring and rendering. I particularly enjoyed developing the monster design. I aimed to create a character that felt eerie but still slightly comedic, aligning with the horror-comedy tone. Experimenting with lighting and texture helped bring the creature to life, even though designing hair as limb-like forms was challenging to pull off convincingly.

In the end, the final comic was completed and prepared for social media publication in time for Halloween

Results

The comic received positive engagement from followers on social media by the time it released. More importantly, this project was a fun learning experience for me in trying out my average art style and interests that I have not tried out before, including complex perspective drawing, visual storytelling (especially aligning with consistent branding tone and content), and atmospheric rendering and composition.

While I am proud of the final result, this project taught me the importance of environmental detail in immersive storytelling. In future comic projects, I would dedicate more time to background development to strengthen atmosphere and eliminate distracting visual elements.
