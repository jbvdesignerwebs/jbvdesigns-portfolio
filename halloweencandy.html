Candy Shortage

Tool: Procreate

Timeline: 1-2 weeks (October 2024)

Background

While working for Ka Manaʻo, we needed to create social media content that would draw more attention to the publication. During the Halloween season, I came up with a couple of comic ideas, and this three-panel comic strip was one of them.

Prior to Process

Before working on this project, I had no experience creating comics, especially not three-panel strips. Storytelling is not usually something I can easily lay out, so I wanted to make sure the idea fit well into a short comic format without boring readers or overloading it with text.

While brainstorming Halloween-themed content with my colleagues, I remembered something funny back when I was in high school: My parents ran out of candy on Halloween and ended up giving trick-or-treaters our leftover boxes of instant ramen packs. Surprisingly, the kids liked it, which inspired me to create a small, lighthearted Halloween comic for the season.

Project Process

Sketching

When sketching the comic, I aimed for a cutesy, old-fashioned style inspired by classic comic strips, but more visually driven than dialogue-heavy. Before drawing, I needed to figure out how to tell the story clearly and consistently within only three panels and minimal dialogue.

After drafting a rough sketch, and getting approval from my colleagues, I created a color thumbnail for the background (though I cannot share it at this time). I then made a semi-detailed sketch focusing on character designs, facial expressions, and shading. After that, I completed the lineart for the characters and background and added the final colors.

Drawing the candy inside the baskets was the most challenging part because I had to differentiate the pieces by color and shape on such a small scale, but I ended up working it through despite major time consumption.

Finalizing

Once the artwork was complete, I designed the text and speech bubble in the first panel to make it more eye-catching and fun. I added lines beneath the bubble to help it stand out even more. I also adjusted the shadows of the trick-or-treaters in the second and third panels by adding purple lines for better clarity. To improve visibility, I changed the lineart colors for the characters, objects, and lighting to contrast with the purple hues, especially in the last two panels.

To emulate older comic strips, I filled in the white borders with typical comic-style text in the gutters, including the title, publication date, and credits for myself and Ka Manaʻo.

Results

Not only did my colleagues appreciate the final comic, but it also received positive engagement from our Instagram followers. Creating this comic strip taught me how important contrast, spacing, and value are when designing comics, especially for readability.
